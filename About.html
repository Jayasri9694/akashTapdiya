About Me

I’m Akash Tapdiya, a poet, storyteller, and listener of the soul’s quiet truths. My words are born from silence, from the raw places of love, loss, healing, and the endless search for meaning. I write to give voice to what often remains unspoken: the ache of longing, the strength of resilience, and the fragile beauty hidden in everyday moments. Nature has always been my teacher. The calm of sunsets, the resilience of the moon, the stillness of the night, they remind me that even silence carries wisdom. Through them, I’ve learned that poetry is not just written but lived. Writing, for me, is sacred. It is not performance but prayer. A bridge to kindred souls. A place where solitude meets connection, where words become a shelter for those who need them most. The bond I share with readers is not about recognition but resonance, that quiet moment when someone whispers, “I’ve felt this too.” My first book Hey! I Know You Can Heal (2021) was a gentle invitation to begin walking inward. With The Unfiltered (2025), I step deeper into truth without disguise, offering not polished verses but the raw pulse of a heart still learning to rise. Beyond words, I hold on to moments through photography, framing emotions the way light holds shadows. Both poetry and photography, for me, are mirrors of the soul, different languages for what cannot always be spoken. If you’ve ever searched for words that feel like home, or carried dreams too heavy for silence, maybe you’ll find a piece of yourself in mine.

The Becoming!
I was not carved, I was called.
Not taught, but tuned by hands I cannot name,
by silence that knew my sound.
This feather - placed, not found. This flute- emptied, not broken.
I do not play - I become what the breath remembers.
Each note is a memory of who I was before forgetting.
Each pause, a prayer carried deep within me.
I am not the music I am its becoming.
A vessel of rhythm, a witness to grace.
He shapes me still not with thunder,
but with touch, not with answers, but with unfolding.
And so I rise, not to perform, but to reveal the version of me
He always knew I could become.
